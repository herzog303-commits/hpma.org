A private community on Harstine Island

Life on the reach.

A gated waterfront community on the quiet east shore of South Puget Sound — fir and madrona, sandstone bluffs, a working marina, and three and a half miles of private beach, kept by the people who live here.

Indian Cove Marina · Hartstene Pointe

One community, cared for in common.

Hartstene Pointe is a private residential community of 532 lots and 90 condominium “Island Houses” on the northern tip of Harstine Island, reached by bridge from the Mason County mainland near Shelton, Washington. Established by Weyerhaeuser in 1970, the Pointe sits on roughly 215 acres, surrounded on three sides by the waters of Puget Sound.

The Maintenance Association keeps the private roads, trails, beaches, marina and shared amenities that make the Pointe what it is — governed by a seven-member Board of Directors elected by the owners and administered by a General Manager and staff.

We hold this shoreline in common — and leave it in better shape than we found it, for the neighbors who come after. — Hartstene Pointe Maintenance Association

Board Meetings

Open meetings, twice monthly

The Board meets on the 2nd Thursday and 3rd Saturday each month. Owner business is conducted at the Saturday meeting — all owners are urged to attend and participate.
